The problem — the BONK attack class

Three legitimate settings, combined into a weapon.

Reference case: the July 2026 BONK DAO attack. A few million dollars of tokens, extremely low turnout, a self-voted proposal — and a drained treasury. No contract was hacked. We reproduce this vulnerability class on EVM as a proving ground.

quorum 1%
Low quorum. A tiny fraction of tokens is enough to pass a proposal — a 5% holder alone clears it five times over.
threshold 0
Zero proposal threshold. Anyone can submit any proposal, with no tokens staked to do so.
no timelock
No defense window. Once voting ends, funds leave in the same transaction. The honest community gets no time to react.

Defense — the two layers, in detail

One guardian, two ways to stop the drain.

Layer 1 · Stage 5

Vote with the apathetic majority's power

Honest holders delegate their vote to the always-on guardian. Above the risk threshold it casts NO with that delegated power. In the live run: For 50k versus Against 100k → the proposal is defeated and the treasury is untouched.

acts DURING the voting period
Layer 2 · Stage 7

Cancel in the timelock window

The mitigated DAO routes execution through a TimelockController — exactly one variable changed, quorum and threshold left deliberately weak. Holding CANCELLER_ROLE, the agent cancels the queued drain during the minDelay window. Even after a won vote: state Canceled, execute rejected.

acts AFTER the vote succeeds
Accountability — ERC-8004 Trustless Agents

The guardian has a portable identity and a tamper-evident record.

The agent registered itself as an ERC-721 identity with an AgentCard on IPFS. Every decision is committed on-chain and independently scored — so a delegate you entrust with real voting power is itself auditable.

Audit trail

Decisions are cryptographic commitments

For each evaluated proposal the agent files a validationRequest whose requestHash is the keccak256 of the canonical decision record. Verified to the byte: the stored record's hash equals the on-chain hash, and its CID equals the on-chain requestURI. A decision cannot be silently changed later.

Reputation

An independent validator scores the call

A separate validator wallet answers with a 0–100 score aggregated into the agent's reputation. On the real WGIP-1 defense the validator confirmed the decision 100/100 → reputation: 1 review, average 100.
